Audience & Purpose

Audience: Beginner anglers who need a quick, practical guide they can use on a phone at the water.

What they already know: Basic gear terms (rod/reel/hook) and the idea of tying a knot, but not which knot to use or how to troubleshoot failures.

What they need: Clear step-by-step instructions, visuals, and quick decision rules that help them choose the right knot/rig without overthinking.

How to Use This Guide

  1. Pick your connection goal (tie on hook, join leader, add lure action).
  2. Follow the steps, then use the “common mistakes” list to self-check.
  3. Wet and tighten slowly, then test with a firm pull before casting.
  4. If something slips, use the troubleshooting section to diagnose quickly.

Gear Checklist

Must-haves

  • Line clippers/scissors
  • Pliers (hook removal + crimping split shot)
  • Small towel (wet knots + grip)

Nice-to-have

  • Leader line (fluoro)
  • Small tackle organizer
  • Extra hooks/weights
